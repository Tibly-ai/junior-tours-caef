Our Mission

Travel that teaches something the textbook can't.

Junior Tours started in 2002 in the back office of a middle school in Portland, where geography teacher Junior Alvarez got tired of watching students memorize capitals they'd never see. He chartered one bus, took eleven kids to Washington D.C., and came home with a waiting list for the next trip.

We still work the same way. Every itinerary starts with a teacher's syllabus or a family's wish list, not a brochure. We build the trip around what the group is studying or curious about, then hand it to a guide who knows the place well enough to answer the questions that come after the official tour ends.

Our guides are teachers, historians, and former students who came back to lead the next group. Most have run twenty or more trips. They know which museum will let a class in before opening and which overlook is worth the extra fifteen minutes of walking.
