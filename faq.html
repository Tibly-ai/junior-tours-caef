FAQ

Before you pack your bag

We hear the same handful of questions on nearly every call, so we wrote the honest answers down. If yours isn't here, call the office and we'll talk it through.

Booking

How far in advance should I book a trip? +

Most small-group departures fill six to ten weeks out, especially our coastal and canyon routes in summer. If you're set on a specific week, book as soon as dates go live. Last-minute openings do happen when someone reschedules, so it's worth a call even when a trip shows sold out.

Can I book if I'm traveling alone? +

You can, and plenty of our travelers do. Groups run 6 to 12 people who mostly haven't met before. We'll pair you with a shared room only if you ask for it. Otherwise you get a single room at the standard supplement.

Safety

What happens if someone gets hurt or sick on the trail? +

Every guide carries a satellite communicator and a full trauma kit. We run a pre-trip call with each traveler to flag allergies or conditions ahead of time, and on multi-day routes we know the nearest evacuation point before we ever leave the trailhead.

Are your guides certified? +

Yes. Lead guides hold Wilderness First Responder certification at minimum, several carry EMT credentials, and we recertify on a two-year cycle. Every guide logs at least 200 days on a route before leading it solo.

Payment

How much deposit do I need to put down? +

A 25% deposit holds your spot, with the balance due 45 days before departure. We take cards, bank transfer, and checks. No booking fee gets tacked on at checkout.

What's your cancellation policy? +

Cancel more than 45 days out and your deposit converts to trip credit, good for two years. Inside 45 days the balance is non-refundable unless we can resell your spot, which we make a real effort to do before writing it off.

Logistics

What's included in the trip price, and what isn't? +

Lodging, guiding, permits, group gear, and most breakfasts and dinners are included. Flights, travel insurance, alcohol, and lunches on free days are on you. Every tour page lists the specifics before you book.

Do I need to bring my own gear? +

We supply group gear such as tents, cooking equipment, and safety kit. You bring personal clothing, boots, and a daypack. We send a packing list four weeks out and can loan trekking poles or a rain shell if you're missing something.
